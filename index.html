Strongest signal: CHIP is seen as uniquely valuable 5 of 9 respondents rate CHIP as "Very valuable — significantly improves our work." All 3 non-users rated it neutrally or poorly, suggesting the gap is in onboarding, not the product itself.
⚠️
Top barrier: data is not up to date Stale data was the #1 challenge cited by CHIP users, and the data delay was rated a "major barrier" by 2 respondents. This was the most consistent complaint across the survey.
🔗
HMIS integration is the #1 feature request 8 of 9 respondents want integration with their national HMIS (e.g. DHIS2). This appeared across all user segments — both CHIP users and non-users.
🎓
Training gaps drive non-adoption All 3 non-users cited lack of training. Even among CHIP users, only 2 of 6 received comprehensive training — the rest had brief orientations or nothing at all.

🚀
Summary conclusion CHIP is valued highly by those who use it. The primary barriers — stale data and lack of training — are the same for both current users and non-users. Addressing these two issues would likely convert neutral or non-users into advocates, and deepen engagement among existing users. The demand for HMIS integration is near-universal and represents the clearest strategic priority for the product roadmap.
